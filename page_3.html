  1. Preheat oven to 375¬∞F (190¬∞C). Line baking sheets with parchment paper.
  2. Cream butter and sugars together in a large bowl until light and fluffy, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Beat in eggs one at a time, then stir in vanilla extract.
  4. Whisk dry ingredients in a separate bowl, then gradually add to the wet mixture, mixing until just combined.
  5. Fold in chocolate chips (and nuts if using) until evenly distributed.
  6. Scoop dough using a tablespoon or cookie scoop, placing balls 2 inches apart on baking sheets.
  7. Bake for 9-11 minutes until edges are golden but centers are still soft.
  8. Cool on sheets for 2 minutes, then transfer to wire racks to cool completely.

üí° Pro Tips

Room temperature ingredients: Ensure butter and eggs are at room temperature for better incorporation.

Don't overmix: Mix just until flour disappears to avoid tough cookies.

Underbake slightly: Cookies will continue cooking on the hot sheet after removal from oven.

Chill the dough: For thicker cookies, refrigerate dough for 30 minutes before baking.

These cookies taste best when enjoyed warm with a glass of cold milk. Store in an airtight container at room temperature for up to 5 days, or freeze for up to 3 months. The dough can also be frozen in balls and baked directly from frozen (add 1-2 minutes to baking time).
